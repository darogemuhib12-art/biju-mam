Chapter One
🎓 First Year — FY

The Beginning of
Everything

Room No. 15. First lecture. I was sitting by the door, at the last desk — completely unbothered. When you walked in, everyone was listening carefully. Not me. I had this thought: "Why should I even listen to all this?"

You started teaching C language — our subject was IP — and honestly, from day one, coding was not my world. I was doing BSc-IT just for the degree. Sitting in class, having fun in the back row, living in my own universe.

You were strict. I was the guy with a chill mind — no filters, just pure natural energy. You noticed me. You even got frustrated with me sometimes. And I remember thinking, "Why is she getting angry?" — not out of ego, just because that's who I genuinely am.

But then — something happened. Day by day, we started talking. And before I even realised it, our bond had quietly grown into something like a mother and son.

Chapter Four — The Last
🎓 Third Year — TY

The Final Chapter,
Forever in the Heart

The Start
Every year I told myself: this time I'll pay attention to every lecture. Every year, the boredom crept back. Not laziness — just a mind that never clicked with code. And yet, somehow, I always showed up.
ASP.NET
The hardest subject in my entire BSc-IT journey. The portion was vast. The concepts were foreign. But I sat through it — and that's something.
The Real Me
Maybe I don't have the "manners" that the world expects. But the honest truth is — I never wanted to be under anyone. My mindset formed that way, for a thousand reasons. And I know, 1000%, it's right for me.
Your Advice
But then I asked you — just once, for a final piece of advice. And you said: "Respect and learn how to talk with people." That's it. Simple. True. I'll get there — not immediately, not eventually — but surely.
Forever
These memories can never fully be put into words. But they will be with me. Till my last breath.
